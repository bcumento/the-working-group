We bring together leaders in the cultural space. Our goal: bringing you more personalized experiences through technological innovations, scalable marketing and data analysis, and aligning company culture.

We work in the arts. We are patron insights experts, web developers, CRM managers, IT professionals, copywriters and designers, working on the frontline of marketing in the cultural space.

Every day we face the same problems you do.

The Working Group’s primary focus is sharing and learning—to benefit the arts sector as a whole. We’re committed to reinvesting in our collective success, sharing scalable solutions that allow all of us to deepen engagement and compete in a fast-changing world.

Whether you want to start an in-depth institution wide project, or simply want to bounce some ideas around and challenge assumptions and start thinking in a different way, we can help.

Why us

The Working Group was founded by New York’s 92nd Street Y.

In an increasingly competitive world, 92nd Street Y recognized five years ago that in order to survive, radical cultural change across the organization was imperative. That meant replacing existing marketing and engagement technology and bringing a new patron-focused strategy to all aspects of the business, significantly reshaping elements of the institution’s structure and dramatically changing the way we engage with our patrons.

We have now developed a roadmap for adopting emerging technology and empowering teams to rapidly respond to the needs of the changing marketplace and the unique challenges faced by nonprofit arts organizations.

Our goal is finding solutions that work. When you join us, you’ll benefit from the experience of and access to the whole 92Y team—from programming to fundraising.

Kent Davidson

Kent Davidson is the Executive Director of The Working Group, and is the Managing Director of Strategic Marketing and Program Administration at 92nd Street Y, where he oversees organization-wide initiatives and programs focused on continuing the institution’s rich history. At 92Y, Kent leads a patron-first approach, overseeing customer care, creative and editorial, CRM, digital content creation and distribution, marketing strategy, enterprise systems and web teams.

Prior to joining 92nd Street Y, Kent served as Managing Director and Director of Marketing and Sales for the North American arm of international ticketing distribution / technology company Seatem, the parent company of Keith Prowse, Globaltickets, ENTA and Ticketswitch. Kent received a B.A. from University of Houston, where he was privileged to learn from some of theater’s top talent, including Edward Albee, Sidney Berger and Sir Peter Hall.
